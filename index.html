was admitted to Nasir Ahmadiyya Muslim Junior School with great determination and seriousness.
In my first term, I performed excellently and took 4th position. I continued to do well in second term, but by third term my focus started changing, though I still managed to gain promotion to Grade 8.
In Grade 8, I completely lost discipline—skipping classes, dodging lessons, and being frequently absent.
Despite this, I relied on my smartness rather than seriousness. During this period, my brother from another parents and roommate was preparing to travel to America, and I followed his unserious lifestyle, forgetting my own future. When he finally left, I failed badly and had to repeat Grade 8.
At the time, it felt like the worst thing, but it later became the turning point of my life.
Repeating Grade 8D exposed me to new classmates who doubted my ability. Though I was still not fully serious, I surprised everyone by performing excellently in first term.I was even offered leadership roles, which I declined.
Eventually, I was promoted to Grade 9A, where I continued to show my intelligence despite inconsistency.
During the exams, I realized my true potential when I passed 7 out of 8 subjects, including Maths and English. However, my lack of discipline nearly cost me my candidature.
This scare pushed me to improve, and with my father’s support, I became more committed. I eventually passed my Grade 9 exams with an aggregate of 28, crediting both Maths and English.
Seeing others perform better motivated me deeply. After Grade 9, I sought guidance from mentors and counselors who helped shape my mindset.
From that moment, I embraced seriousness and commitment, and since then, I have been improving continuously.
"Alhamdulillah", my journey is now one of growth, learning, and transformation.
